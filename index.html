HILO: A Large-Scale Heterogeneous Object Dataset for Benchmarking Robotic Grasping Approaches

Published in ICARA 2025

Xinchao Song, Sean Banerjee, and Natasha Kholgade Banerjee

Terascale All sensing Research Studio

Paper Available Soon Dataset Code


Abstract

Robust object manipulation is critical for robotics applications in real-world environments, especially when handling diverse and complex everyday objects. To facilitate this research, we present HILO, a large-scale dataset of 253 everyday objects that bridges a crucial gap in existing manipulation datasets through its heterogeneity and unique dual-resolution approach. By combining high-resolution individual object scans with low-resolution scans of cluttered scenes, HILO provides both the precise geometric data needed for grasp planning and realistic environmental context. The dataset's comprehensive representations enable rigorous benchmarking of robotic grasping algorithms. Our evaluation of three leading grasping algorithms reveals trade-offs between grasp quantity and quality, demonstrating the dataset's value in advancing manipulation research. HILO's rich object diversity and dual-resolution methodology provide a foundation for developing more versatile robotic systems capable of reliable real-world manipulation..

Dataset Scenes

The HILO dataset contains 288 total scenes. Each scene contains:
  • 120 or 104 raw RGBD images for 10 different objects
  • 120 or 104 corresponding undistorted RGBD images
  • Masks for the undistorted RGBD images
  • Pose annotations
